Blog: Quantum Entanglement

This introductory wave demonstrates how two‑qubit systems exhibit entanglement.

A Bell pair carries one bit of entanglement entropy and violates the CHSH inequality up to $2\sqrt{2}$.

Random states rarely reach that maximum; their Schmidt coefficients reveal partial entanglement.

The entropy formula $S=-\sum \lambda_i \log_2 \lambda_i$ applied to the Bell state gives $S=1$, while a state with coefficients $(0.944,0.329)$ yields $S\approx0.78$.

For background reading see arXiv:quant-ph/0101012.
