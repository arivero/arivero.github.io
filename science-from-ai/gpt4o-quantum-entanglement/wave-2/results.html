Results: Quantum Entanglement

Teleportation simulations of 100 random qubits achieved mean fidelity $F\approx1$, demonstrating a correct implementation of the Bennett protocol and faultless classical feed-forward.

Tracing out two qubits of a GHZ state produced a maximally mixed single-qubit density matrix with von Neumann entropy of $1$ bit, quantitatively capturing its tripartite entanglement.

Entanglement swapping starting from two Bell pairs yielded an output Bell state with concurrence near unity, confirming that long-distance entanglement can be generated through local Bell measurements.

Reference: arXiv:quant-ph/0101012
