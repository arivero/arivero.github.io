Blog: Mathmo Interests

This blog offers informal glimpses into assorted mathematical explorations—combinatorics, geometry, number theory, and more.

The first entries discuss Catalan number gadgets and intuition behind hyperbolic space drawings.

For example, the Catalan generating function $A(x)=\frac{1-\sqrt{1-4x}}{2x}$ expands to $1+x+2x^2+5x^3+\cdots$, matching the counts of Dyck paths used in the daily logs.

In hyperbolic puzzles, geodesics are circles orthogonal to the disk boundary; measuring their lengths with the metric $ds=\frac{2|dz|}{1-|z|^2}$ reveals counterintuitive shortest routes.
