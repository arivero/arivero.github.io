Daily Run: Connes' Standard Model

Day 1

Subtopics: We began this wave with three lines of inquiry: the $a_4$ heat-kernel coefficient on $S^4$, the CKM matrix's Jarlskog invariant, and gauge coupling unification.

$a_4$ coefficient on $S^4$

  1. Consulted arXiv:hep-th/0610241 and downloaded the PDF for reference.
  2. Inserted the curvature invariants of the unit four-sphere into the Seeley--DeWitt formula to express $a_4$ in terms of $R^2$.
  3. Used a short SymPy script to evaluate the expression numerically, yielding $a_4=1/10$.

CKM Jarlskog invariant

  1. Constructed the CKM matrix with Wolfenstein parameters $(\lambda,A,\bar{\rho},\bar{\eta})=(0.225,0.8,0.135,0.349)$.
  2. Computed the Jarlskog invariant $J=A^2\lambda^6\bar{\eta}\approx3.0\times10^{-5}$.
  3. Verified CP violation by confirming that $J$ is nonzero and of the expected magnitude.

Gauge coupling unification

  1. Integrated the one-loop renormalisation group equations up to $10^{16}\,\text{GeV}$ using initial values at $M_Z$.
  2. Observed $g_1$, $g_2$ and $g_3$ approach within $5\%$ of a common value near the unification scale.
  3. Noted the residual mismatch suggesting threshold corrections are required.

Subtopic assessment: Each subtopic produced concrete numbers: the heat-kernel coefficient and Jarlskog invariant matched expectations, while the coupling unification highlighted a gap for future work.
